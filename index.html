The problem: healthcare is reactive

Quick question — how many of you only visit a doctor after Google has already scared you with 27 possible diseases?

That’s the problem. Healthcare today is reactive. We wait until things break. Diseases like diabetes or heart issues don’t send a warning email — they sneak in quietly. In fact, WHO says they caused 43 million deaths in 2021 alone.

  • Fragmented data: devices don't talk to clinician systems.
  • Late action: care happens after deterioration.
  • Information overload: clinicians drown in unusable signals.

Workflow — how it works

Simple, reliable flow from device → cloud → clinician → patient. We keep clinicians in the loop while automating baseline detection and alerts.

1. Device captures vitals

Wearables, rings, bedside hubs collect heart rate, SpO₂, activity and sleep.

2. Local preprocessing & ingestion

Edge smoothing and secure ingestion (MQTT/HTTPS) into the cloud.

3. Time-series storage & features

Store raw signals and derived features in a time-series DB for robust analysis.

4. AI core — personalized baselines

Rolling averages + z-scores compare each patient to their own baseline to reduce false alarms.

5. Clinician-in-the-loop & voice hub

AI proposes risk cards; clinicians review and patients receive voice or push updates.

Tech stack & approach

Taken from the project notes (shortened): Devices simulated with Python; secure ingestion via MQTT/HTTPS; backend built with FastAPI; time-series storage using InfluxDB and SQLite backup; AI using rolling averages + z-scores and scikit-learn; dashboard prototypes with Streamlit; voice via Web Speech API; security: HTTPS + JWT.
